SAIT Trades & Technology: 750,000 sq ft of Kalzip

A LEED-accredited expansion to Calgary's SAIT campus — Kalzip standing seam delivered the performance, geometry, and sustainability the project required across 750,000 square feet.

// FIG. 01 — SAIT TRADES & TECHNOLOGY / 750,000 SQ FT KALZIP

The Southern Alberta Institute of Technology (SAIT) Trades & Technology Complex represents one of the largest single applications of Kalzip standing seam in Canadian institutional architecture. The project's envelope strategy was driven by three converging requirements: LEED accreditation, complex curved geometry, and survival under Calgary's extreme thermal cycling.

The Specification

Kalzip XT Standing Seam was specified across 750,000 sq ft of roof and wall surface. On-site mobile XT roll-formers eliminated panel transport length limitations, enabling the seamless long panels that the building geometry required.

"On-site roll-forming eliminated transport length limitations — and the E-Clip thermal break delivered measurable energy improvement."

— Project Engineering Team

Thermal Performance

The E-Clip thermal break system was central to the project's LEED accreditation strategy. By reducing conductive heat loss through cladding fasteners by approximately 80%, E-Clip delivered measurable energy performance improvement over conventional fastener-through systems — a meaningful contribution to the project's effective wall R-value.

Calgary Climate

Calgary's climate cycles between extreme cold (-30°C) and warm chinook events (+15°C) within hours. Kalzip's aluminium substrate handles this thermal cycling without the fatigue cracking that affects steel standing-seam systems over time.

Project Highlights

  • 750,000 sq ft Kalzip XT Standing Seam
  • On-site XT roll-forming for unlimited panel length
  • E-Clip thermal break — 80% reduction in fastener heat loss
  • LEED-accredited envelope
  • Aluminium substrate — fatigue resistance for chinook cycling
