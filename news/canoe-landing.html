Canoe Landing Campus: A $65M Community Anchor

Three L-shaped buildings clad in 6mm Alpolic ACP define this innovative downtown Toronto campus housing schools, recreation, and childcare on a single CityPlace site.

// FIG. 01 — CANOE LANDING CAMPUS / ALPOLIC ACP

Located in downtown Toronto's CityPlace residential neighbourhood and designed by ZAS Architects Inc, Canoe Landing houses a $65M community recreation centre, public and Catholic elementary schools, and a childcare centre — all within one innovative campus.

The three L-shaped buildings at Canoe Landing Campus are clad in Alpolic aluminium composite panels (ACP). The ACP material was specified for its dimensional stability, custom finish capability, and proven track record on similar institutional projects across Ontario.

Three Buildings, One Aesthetic

The campus design uses Alpolic's consistent finish quality to unify three distinct programmatic buildings under a single architectural language. The L-shaped massings interlock around shared outdoor space — but the unified cladding strategy reads as one campus rather than three buildings.

"Houses a $65M community recreation centre, public and Catholic elementary schools, and a childcare centre within one innovative campus."

— Project Brief

Specification Detail

The project used 6mm ALPOLIC ACP throughout — heavier-gauge than the more typical 4mm to support the complex panel geometry and span between the steel sub-frame supports. Custom finishes were specified to match the project's civic identity.
