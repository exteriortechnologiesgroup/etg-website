TTC York University Station: Free-Form Aluminium

AECOM & Aedas's 407 TTC subway station combines Kalzip XT Standing Seam with Radius Track curved framing for a stunning free-form aluminium roof that won the 2017 CanBIM GC Award.

// FIG. 01 — TTC YORK STATION / FREE-FORM KALZIP + RADIUS TRACK

The 407 TTC Subway Station by AECOM & Aedas required a free-form aluminium roof — geometry that defied conventional sub-frame construction methods. The solution combined two ETG-represented products into a single integrated system.

This is the Free-Form Aluminium Roof on 407 TTC Subway Station. To achieve this beautiful design, Kalzip XT Standing Seam Roof and Radius Track Curved Framing were used on top of regular roof deck.

BIM-Driven Manufacturing

Radius Track's manufacturing process accepts native Revit and Rhino models as input. The Aedas geometric model was transferred directly into Radius Track's production queue — every framing component pre-cut and pre-curved to project-specific geometry, then numbered and sequenced for installation.

"To achieve this beautiful design, Kalzip XT Standing Seam Roof and Radius Track Curved Framing were used on top of regular roof deck."

— ETG Project Brief

Field Installation

Kalzip XT Standing Seam panels installed onto the Radius Track curved sub-frame using the standard E-Clip system — but with custom panel curvatures generated by the same on-site XT roll-former that enabled the SAIT project. The combined system delivered the architectural intent without field-bending or installer rework.

Industry Recognition

The combined system won the 2017 CanBIM GC Award — recognition specifically for the design-to-fabrication BIM workflow that made this geometry economically achievable. The win underscored a broader industry shift toward integrated BIM-native fabrication for complex geometry.

System Highlights

  • Free-form aluminium roof geometry
  • Kalzip XT Standing Seam — primary roofing
  • Radius Track Curved-Right framing — sub-structure
  • Native Revit/Rhino BIM workflow
  • 2017 CanBIM GC Award
  • AECOM / Aedas — design lead
