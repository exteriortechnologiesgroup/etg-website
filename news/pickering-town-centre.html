Pedestrian Bridge in Pickering: A Landmark in Modern Bridge Design

A landmark in modern Canadian bridge design, featuring ALPOLIC composite materials and innovative cladding strategies that blend performance with civic identity.

// FIG. 01 — PICKERING PEDESTRIAN BRIDGE / ALPOLIC CLADDING

The Pickering Pedestrian Bridge in Pickering, Ontario represents a new chapter in Canadian infrastructure design — a structure that prioritizes user experience and civic identity alongside engineering performance.

The bridge's cladding strategy uses ALPOLIC composite materials combined with sophisticated rainscreen detailing to manage solar exposure, water shed, and long-term durability across one of Canada's most weather-variable environments.

Cladding Strategy

Public infrastructure imposes uniquely demanding cladding requirements: 50+ year service life, vandalism resistance, repairability, and aesthetic permanence across decades of public use. ALPOLIC met every criterion.

"A landmark in modern Canadian bridge design — a structure that prioritizes user experience alongside engineering performance."

— Project Brief

Material Selection

The team specified ALPOLIC for several reasons: dimensional stability across thermal cycles, repairability of individual panels (vs continuous skin systems), proven Canadian climate performance, and the ability to deliver custom finish profiles within an institutional budget envelope.

The bridge represents the kind of project where ETG's pre-construction partnership delivers tangible value — material specification, AutoCAD detailing, and field review across the full project timeline.
