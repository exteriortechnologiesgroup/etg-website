Zinc Roofing in Cold Climates: Design and Durability

Zinc's impermeability makes it impervious to the freeze-thaw cycles that damage other roofing materials. A technical overview for Canadian climate conditions.

// FIG. 01 — ELZINC ROOFING IN CANADIAN COLD CLIMATE

Zinc's impermeability makes it impervious to the freeze-thaw cycles that can damage other roofing materials. Once installed, zinc remains unfazed by low temperatures and enjoys a long life — exactly the performance profile Canadian building envelopes demand.

Freeze-Thaw Immunity

Conventional roofing materials degrade under freeze-thaw cycling because they absorb moisture, then experience destructive expansion as that water freezes. Zinc's dense titanium alloy surface does not absorb water — eliminating the mechanism of damage at its source.

Even when snow accumulates on zinc roofs, the surface finish remains largely unaffected. However, after particularly snowy winters, a temporary white discolouration may appear as the snow melts and reveals retained meltwater. This effect gradually fades back to the normal colour of zinc.

"This effect is absent in finishes like elZinc Rainbow or elZinc Advance — making them ideal for the most demanding Canadian projects."

— elZinc Technical Brief

Why elZinc

The full Alkimi range carries a lifetime warranty against finish defects — a guarantee that's only possible with confidence in long-term performance.

Range of Finishes for Cold Climate

  • elZinc Natural — develops natural patina over 18-24 months of weather exposure
  • elZinc Alkimi — six pre-patinated greys (Slate, Graphite, Crystal, Lava, etc.)
  • elZinc Rainbow — six coloured variants immune to white discolouration
  • elZinc Advance — proprietary finish with enhanced colour stability

Specification Considerations

For Canadian cold-climate projects, key specification considerations include: standing seam vs flat-lock detail (drainage and ice management), substrate compatibility (avoid copper contact), thermal expansion accommodation (typical 0.022 mm/m/°C), and ventilated assembly behind the zinc to manage moisture.
