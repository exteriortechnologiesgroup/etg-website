Sheraton Halifax: Atlantic Coast Cladding

Marine-grade cladding for an Atlantic-coast hospitality landmark, balancing salt exposure resistance with refined aesthetics for the harbour-facing façade.

// FIG. 01 — SHERATON HALIFAX / MARINE-GRADE CLADDING

Atlantic-coast hospitality projects impose unusually demanding requirements on cladding systems. Salt spray exposure, hurricane-grade wind loads, and freeze-thaw cycling combine to accelerate failure of any system that's not specifically engineered for marine environments.

The Sheraton Halifax project illustrates how careful material selection — informed by ETG's pre-construction technical consultation — can deliver decade-spanning performance even in this aggressive environment.

The Challenge

Halifax's harbour-facing buildings see direct salt aerosol deposition during winter storms. Conventional galvanized steel cladding can show measurable surface corrosion within 5-7 years of installation. Even some "marine-grade" finishes fail to deliver the 25-30 year service life that institutional and hospitality clients increasingly expect.

"Marine-grade cladding for an Atlantic-coast hospitality landmark — balancing salt exposure resistance with refined aesthetics."

— Project Brief

Material Strategy

The cladding strategy combined ALPOLIC composite panels for the primary façade with concealed-fastener detailing throughout. The PVDF finish delivers exceptional resistance to salt-spray corrosion, while the aluminium substrate is fundamentally immune to red rust.

Performance Highlights

  • ALPOLIC primary cladding — PVDF finish
  • Stainless-steel concealed clip system (316 marine grade)
  • Hurricane wind-load engineered sub-frame
  • Drained & ventilated rainscreen detail
  • 20-year finish warranty
