What is an EPD (Environmental Product Declaration)?

Sustainability is at the core of modern architecture, and Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs) play a crucial role in assessing the environmental impact of building materials. An EPD is an independently verified document that provides transparent and comparable data on the life-cycle environmental impact of a product. These declarations are widely recognized in green building certification programs such as LEED and BREEAM, making them essential tools for sustainable construction.

elZinc®'s Latest EPDs: A Step Toward a Greener Future

As a leader in sustainable metal cladding, elZinc® has renewed its Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) for elZinc Natural and introduced a new EPD for its entire range of elZinc Alkimi® pre-weathered finishes under the GlobalEPD programme of AENOR. This initiative reflects elZinc®'s dedication to environmental responsibility and innovation in zinc-based roofing and cladding solutions.

These EPDs provide architects and builders with validated information on the environmental performance of elZinc® products, reinforcing their suitability for green building projects. The availability of such certifications ensures that clients working with Exterior Technologies Group can specify materials that contribute to environmentally responsible construction while maintaining superior aesthetics and performance.
