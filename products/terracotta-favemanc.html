// PRODUCT OVERVIEW

Ancient material.
Modern envelope.

Favemanc is the architectural facing brand of the Gresmanc Group, Spain's leading manufacturer of extruded clinker ceramics. The result is a family of terracotta cladding products that combine the warmth and permanence of fired ceramic with the engineered precision of a contemporary rainscreen.

Extruded clinker is fundamentally different from compressed terracotta or porcelain tile. The clay is pressed through a die at high pressure, producing dense profiles with a hollow honeycomb core — this is what gives Favemanc panels their strength-to-weight ratio. The panels are then fired at over 1,150°C, vitrifying the body and locking in colour permanently.

Because colour is in the clay itself — not a glaze, paint, or coating — Favemanc panels are UV-stable, freeze-thaw resistant, and entirely maintenance-free. The honeycomb core also provides natural thermal insulation properties and reduces the panel's installed weight by approximately 30% compared to solid terracotta of equivalent dimensions.

The Favemanc range covers three primary system families: flat rainscreen panels for vertical façades; baguette and brise-soleil profiles for solar shading, screening, and sculptural detailing; and ceramic sunscreen louver systems for full-height façade treatments. All three families can be combined within a single project to create dimensional, dynamic envelopes.

ETG distributes Favemanc across Canada with full pre-construction support — substructure coordination, cold-climate detailing, anchor selection, and panel-layout optimization. From civic and educational projects to multi-family residential and commercial towers, Favemanc gives Canadian architects access to the depth, texture, and warmth that only fired terracotta can deliver.
