TAC Thermal Spacer

97% Reduction in Thermal Bridging

TAC Thermal Spacer is an affordable solution to effectively reduce thermal bridging by at least 97% for rainscreen exterior wall systems. Three configurations — fiberglass, galvanized steel, and clip with girt — meet building code thermal performance requirements.

System &
Performance

Conventional rainscreen sub-frames create thermal bridges that bypass insulation and degrade effective R-value by 30-60%. TAC's engineered clip eliminates this conduction path while maintaining structural integrity at full code wind loads.

// FEAT 01

TAC 1.0 Fiberglass

Composite fiberglass clip — 97% thermal break with structural capacity for typical metal panel cladding.

// FEAT 02

Galvanized steel hybrid clip — increased load capacity for heavier cladding (terracotta, stone veneer).

TAC 2.1 with Girt

Pre-assembled clip + girt system — accelerated install, reduced field labour, consistent thermal performance.

// CASE STUDY — PAN AM AQUATICS

97% Reduction, Verified at Scale

The Pan Am Aquatic Centre in Toronto required a rainscreen wall system that could meet aggressive thermal performance targets while supporting heavy mineral-fibre exterior insulation. Conventional Z-girt sub-frames would have degraded effective R-value by an unacceptable 35-40%.

TAC Thermal Spacer 2.0 was specified across the full façade. The fiberglass-composite clip eliminated the thermal bridge while delivering full code wind uplift capacity — verified through third-party hot box testing.
